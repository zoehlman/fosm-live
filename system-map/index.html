The Creation Code — FOSM·LIVE canonical reference · v0.6 · the source of truth every other view defers to
★ canon source of truth
FOSM·LIVE is not a management framework. It is the Creation Code. The structural grammar that anything alive — a person, a couple, a team, a company, a society — must enact in order to exist, sustain, and create. The eight dimensions are not invented by FOSM·LIVE; they are recognized by it. They appear in a single person tending their own life. They appear in a family raising children. They appear in a team shipping work. They appear in a company serving customers. They appear in a nation organizing its people. The same eight, every time, because they are what is required for any viable thing to keep being viable. This view is the canonical reference — when something in the product disagrees with what's here, the product is wrong, not the reference, until the reference is deliberately revised. Every other view in this map is an instantiation; this one is the grammar.

Why the split is load-bearing
Functions are flow objects — queried as time-series. Capacities are pattern objects — queried as longitudinal observations (commitments tracked, decisions logged, vision articulations sampled). The product data model enforces this — they live in different shapes.

Sales and Marketing are formally distinct. Sales is downstream of the commit-to-exchange boundary; Marketing is upstream of it. Treating them as one dimension is one of the most common diagnostic mistakes in operational management — it hides which side of the boundary is failing.
The recursion · same shape at every scale— precise term: holon (Koestler) · informally: fractal · what differs is vocabulary, not grammar
Every viable unit in FOSM·LIVE is the same type of object — a Viable Unit — with the same eight dimensions and the same internal logic. The only difference between a person and a company in the data model is that one contains the other. This is not a metaphor — it is the literal database schema. The technical term is holon (Arthur Koestler, 1967): something simultaneously a whole and a part. A person is a whole human being and also a part of a team. A team is a whole and also a part of a department. A viable holon honors both faces — has integrity as itself and serves the larger whole.
The Recursion Test
If a sub-unit does not contain its own functioning instance of all eight dimensions, it is structurally incomplete. A department with no internal vision function is a structural fault, not a stylistic choice.
The Mirroring Requirement
Health at one scale requires health at adjacent scales. A company cannot have integrity if its departments do not. A person cannot have integrity if their internal commitments do not align.
The Single Engine
The diagnostic logic FOSM·LIVE applies to a person is the same logic it applies to a team or a company. Building it once is building it forever.

The diagnostic decision tree · the algorithm FOSM·LIVE runs— state in, diagnosis out · applies to any Viable Unit at any scale
Inputs: the Viable Unit, its 8-dimension state, parent state, child states, recent trends.

Step 1 · Triage. Any dimension in critical state (Cash Starvation P-19, or other survival-threatening pattern within the unit's cycle)? Return that with urgency flag. Preempt all else.
Step 2 · Lowest-scoring dimension. Find it. Also compute function-group avg (F·O·S·M) vs. capacity-group avg (L·I·V·E). Capacity weakness with healthy functions → suspect P-10 Capacity Hollowing.
Step 3 · Triangle check. Inspect the S↔M↔O boundaries explicitly for P-15 to P-18. Common, often misdiagnosed.
Step 4 · Recurse downward. Is the weak dimension also weak in child units? Rooted at this scale or below.
Step 5 · Recurse upward. Is the weak dimension also weak in parent? May be inherited from above.
Step 6 · Recursion Test. Does the unit contain functioning instances of all 8 dimensions? If not, P-01 Recursion Fault.
Step 7 · Pattern-match. Profile against the library's detection signatures. Rank matches by specificity.
Step 8 · Output. Primary pathology, scale-of-root-cause, scale-of-intervention, intervention class, confidence score. If confidence < 0.6, surface 2 alternatives.

The Theory of Impact · how the framework reads reality— retrodictive measurement · peace + prosperity as destination · typical-patterns-not-personal-prophecy
The destination · peace + prosperity
The framework is opinionated about exactly one thing: the destination. Peace is the internal state — the integration in which parts are not at war and the whole can rest. Prosperity is the external state — sufficiency of material, relational, meaning-bearing resources. Every wisdom tradition that has taken human flourishing seriously converges on this cluster. The framework does not invent this destination; it recognizes the convergence.
The method · retrodictive measurement
Like climate science. Like epidemiology. Take what actually happened, find the patterns that fit history, use them — humbly, with confidence intervals — to understand what choices tend to produce what outcomes. The honest form: "For people with patterns like yours, here is what typically unfolds — and here are the points where the pattern bent for those who bent it." The dishonest form is fortune-telling. The framework only ever speaks in the first voice.
The self-iteration loop
A framework that cannot update against reality is dead on arrival. Every diagnosis is a prediction to be tested. Where prediction and outcome agree, the model is confirmed. Where they disagree, the model is updated — refined parameters, new pattern identified, or the case acknowledged as outside what the framework currently models. Predictive accuracy is tracked by domain, by scale, by pathology. Users have a right to see how confident the framework is and where it has been wrong before.
Goodhart's Law and the discipline against it. When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. Every metric in FOSM·LIVE is a proxy — a finger pointing at the moon, not the moon. Three protections: multiple proxies for any quality, explicit acknowledgment in every output of what the proxy is measuring and where it fails, and a discipline of returning to the destination — peace and prosperity — and asking whether the unit is actually moving toward it. A unit whose metrics improve while the felt experience of being inside it degrades has not improved. The framework knows this and says so.

The moment the framework declares a choice good or bad, half the room stops listening. So the framework refuses both moves. What it does instead is measure. Every choice produces something. The judgment belongs to the people inside, looking at what their arrangements have actually produced. The framework is opinionated about the measurement structure and humble about the user's choices.
